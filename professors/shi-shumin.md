## Biography
The Office of International Affairs and Cooperation is the university's functional department and administrative office responsible for implementing foreign-related policies and coordinating international exchange and cooperation affairs. Its main responsibilities include: drafting the university's development plan for international cooperation and exchanges; coordinating the university's international activities, providing information and liaison services for the teaching, scientific research, and international academic exchanges of colleges and departments; planning and implementing major visits, receptions, and foreign-related activities; overall management of university-level exchange programs, including program formulation, agreement drafting, implementation of exchange projects, and tracking management and services; recruitment, approval, and management of foreign experts' affairs and daily life; review and approval of international conferences; liaison, review, and approval for overseas programs; handling passports and visas (permits) for faculty and staff on official temporary trips abroad (overseas) and students on official trips abroad, and providing consultation for faculty and staff on private trips abroad (overseas); overall management of students' long-term and short-term overseas programs, international internships, international competitions, and international summer (winter) camps; exchanges and cooperation with Hong Kong, Macao, and Taiwan regions; and other foreign affairs tasks assigned by the university.

The International Student Center is a university-level unit responsible for the enrollment and management of various types of international students and specialized in teaching Chinese as a foreign language and research. It is also a test center for the Chinese Proficiency Test (HSK). Its main responsibilities include: formulating development plans, enrollment plans, and management regulations for international students; recruitment, promotion, consultation, and admission of international students; handling visa applications, residence permits, accommodation registration, insurance purchase, and other entry-exit procedures for international students, as well as daily life management, academic management, and social management; evaluation and daily management of Chinese Government Scholarships, Beijing Municipal Government Scholarships for International Students, and university scholarships for international students; academic record management, file management, and issuance of graduation (completion) certificates and degree certificates for international students; teaching Chinese as a foreign language, research, and training of Chinese language teachers; organizing international students to take the HSK; organizing international students to participate in Chinese cultural experiences and social practice activities; and other tasks related to international students assigned by the university.
